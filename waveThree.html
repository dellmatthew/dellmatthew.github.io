Third Wave Emo

Mall Emo

Third wave Emo sees the expansion of Emo into the mainstream. In the early 2000’s, many record labels began to pick up many unnoticed artists inside the genre, bands such as Jimmy Eat World grew a steady following that spread mostly through word of mouth. Then, labels began promoting these bands like crazy, having them open for massive punk bands like Green Day and Weezer. These new bands started to incorporate a more “poppy” sound to their tracks

This leads into the summer of 2002 where Emo and stereotypical emo fashion went wild. The launch of MySpace is what allowed emo to get so mainstream. Bands like My Chemical Romance, Paramore, Fall Out Boy, and Panic! At the Disco dominated the scene. These bands and their style became iconic, often dubbed “mall emo”. They had black hair, skinny jeans, chains, and eyeliner. These bands continued to explore themes of heartbreak, mental health, and self identification. These themes resonated heavily in the teenage youth at the time and the Emo subculture was formed for people who felt rejected or different from everyone around them.

With this being the most commercially successful iteration of Emo, it attracted tons of controversy both within and outside of the community. People outside of the community viewed insiders as sensitive, and overtly whiney. Similarly, mall emo was disliked by some due to it being too commercial and detached from the true emotional roots of emo music.
