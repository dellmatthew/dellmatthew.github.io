Fourth Wave Emo

Emo Revival

Fourth Wave is also named Emo Revival and it is more of a return to the first and second wave emo roots. It combines the elements of midwest emo, math rock, and post rock. This wave has notable artists such as Modern Baseball, Mom Jeans, Title Fight, and Joyce Manor. These bands mainly took inspiration from the already established midwest emo scene and had a focus on this DIY sound. The sound of fourth wave emo more or less stayed consistent with that of the previous waves. There was a major focus on underground emo music during this time, with independent artists performing basement shows and as openers for more commercial emo bands. This era was like a revival and a tribute to the original sound of first and second wave emo. Additionally bands like American football and the Get-Up Kids began touring again. One of the most important aspects of this wave is the creation of emo subgeneres, such as Emo Rap, that became extremely popular in the mid 2010’s.

Emo rap is a subgenera of both hip-hop and emo. It emerged in the 2010’s in the Soundcloud era of hip hop. Soundcloud made independent music creation very accessible to almost anyone. Artists such as Juice World, Lil Peep, and XXXTentacion were extremely well known in the genre. The sound of Emo Rap blended together minimalist melancholic beats often mixed with guitar or piano looped melodies, and the lyrics would incorporate the artist’s feelings of loneliness and isolation. Many themes included drug use, heartbreak, and alienation.
