First Wave Emo

The genre of Emo began in the 1980’s in Washington DC, branching off of the hardcore and post-hardcore/punk scene. The first instance of an “Emo” band was the band Rites of Spring. This band blended together the raw intense sound of hardcore punk music with their deeply personal “emotional” lyrics. The lyrics have a major importance in shaping the genre as a whole, the lyrics are super personal and confessional, they explore complex topics surrounding brain turmoil and relationship struggles. In addition to the deep lyrics, first wave Emo had an upbeat dynamic song structure with sudden tempo changes, aggressive guitar/drum bursts. This primal iteration of Emo ultimately did not have any commercial success outside of the Washington DC region.
