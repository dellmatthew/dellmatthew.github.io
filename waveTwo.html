Second Wave Emo

Midwest Emo

Second wave emo is most commonly associated with “Midwest Emo” and begins as Emo leaves the Washington DC scene and begins to spread around the United States. Specifically the Midwest region of the US, hence its given name. At the time, the name referenced a specific region of Emo music, however it was later adapted to represent this second wave of Emo music as a whole, and Midwest Emo evolved to represent a subgenera of sorts within Emo music.

Similarly to its first wave roots, Midwest Emo continues to focus on the personal heart-wrenching topics that formed the genre. Singers would often perform in a way that diverged from traditional songwriting structures and create these pieces akin to lyrical poems. With inspirations from punk bands like The Smiths and Fugazi, bands such as Sunny Day Real Estate, American Football, and many more formed. The style these new bands invented focused on the distinct whiney vocal and guitar melodies and were detaching from the hardcore roots that the genre was founded on.

At this point Emo is earning some of its stereotypes, with that of nerdy sensitive boys and girls that whine and mope over instruments.

The time period of Midwest Emo is marked throughout the 1990’s and it’s agreed that wave two Emo ends once the commercial success of Emo begins, after finally breaking into mainstream media in the summer of 2002.
